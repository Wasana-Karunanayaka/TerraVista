Must-Visit Destinations in Morocco

A land of dizzying diversity, from snow-capped mountains to sweeping desert dunes.

Marrakech

The "Red City." Explore the chaotic Jemaa el-Fnaa square, intricate palaces, and the vibrant Majorelle Garden.

Chefchaouen

The "Blue Pearl." A mountain city famous for its striking blue-washed buildings and relaxed atmosphere.

Fes

The cultural capital. Home to the world's oldest university and the massive, labyrinthine medina (Fes el Bali).

The Sahara

Ride camels into the sunset at Erg Chebbi (Merzouga) and sleep under a blanket of stars in a Berber camp.

Casablanca

The modern economic hub. Visit the awe-inspiring Hassan II Mosque, one of the largest in the world.

Atlas Mountains

North Africa's highest mountain range. Perfect for trekking, visiting Berber villages, and seeing snow in Africa.

Essaouira

A windy coastal fortress town known for its fresh seafood, Gnaoua music festival, and Game of Thrones filming.

Ait Benhaddou

A UNESCO earthen fortified village (Ksar) along the former caravan route. A famous backdrop for Gladiator.

Moroccan Mosaic

An enchanting mix of Arab, Berber, and European influences.

🧎

Maghrebi Mint Tea

Known as "Berber Whiskey." Poured from a height to create froth, it's a symbol of hospitality served at every opportunity.

🛍

The Souks

An assault on the senses. Markets filled with spices, rugs, leather, and lamps. Bargaining is mandatory!

🍛

Tagine & Couscous

Tagine is the conical clay pot and the slow-cooked stew inside. Couscous is the national dish, traditionally served on Fridays.

🛀

Hammam

The traditional steam bath. A weekly ritual of deep cleansing using distinct black soap (Savon Beldi) and scrubbing.

🪠

Zellige (Mosaic)

Exquisite geometric tilework found on fountains, walls, and floors. A mastercraft of Islamic art and patience.
