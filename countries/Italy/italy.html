An Open-Air Museum

Home to the most UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the world.

Rome (Roma)

The Eternal City. Step back in time at the Colosseum and Roman Forum, and throw a coin into the Trevi Fountain.

Venice (Venezia)

The Floating City. A marvel of engineering built on lagoons. Ride a gondola through the Grand Canal and visit St. Mark's Square.

Florence (Firenze)

Cradle of the Renaissance. Home to Michelangelo's David, the Uffizi Gallery, and the iconic Duomo.

Amalfi Coast

A vertical landscape. Dramatic cliffs, pastel-colored villages like Positano, and the glamorous island of Capri nearby.

Cinque Terre

Five Lands. Five colorful fishing villages perched on rugged cliffs, connected by scenic hiking trails and trains.

Vatican City

The smallest country in the world (inside Rome). See the Sistine Chapel ceiling by Michelangelo and St. Peter's Basilica.

Pompeii

Frozen in time. An ancient Roman city buried by the ash of Mt. Vesuvius in 79 AD, offering a unique glimpse into the past.

Milan (Milano)

Fashion & Finance. The global capital of design and fashion, home to the massive Gothic Duomo and Da Vinci's Last Supper.

Sicily

The crossroads of the Mediterranean. Ancient Greek valleys of temples, Mt. Etna volcano, and distinct street food.

Italian Lifestyle

Food, Art, and "La Dolce Vita".

🍝

Culinary Art

Food is sacred. Every region has specialties (Pizza in Naples, Carbonara in Rome). Meals are long, social events.

🛵

La Dolce Vita

"The Sweet Life." A philosophy of enjoying life's pleasures: good food, good company, and taking time to relax.

☕

Espresso Rules

Coffee is drunk standing at the bar. Cappuccino is strictly for breakfast; ordering one after 11 AM is a tourist sin.

🎨

Art & Architecture

Italy holds the greatest number of UNESCO sites. The legacy of Da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael is visible everywhere.

🚶

La Passeggiata

The evening stroll. Between 5 PM and 8 PM, locals dress up and walk through the main streets to socialize and be seen.
