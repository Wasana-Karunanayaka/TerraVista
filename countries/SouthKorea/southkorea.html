Must-Visit Destinations in South Korea

A perfect blend of ancient tradition and hyper-modern innovation.

Seoul

The dynamic capital. Visit Gyeongbokgung Palace, N Seoul Tower, and the trendy streets of Gangnam.

Busan

Korea's "Miami," famous for Haeundae Beach, fresh seafood markets, and the colorful Gamcheon Culture Village.

Jeju Island

A volcanic island paradise with waterfalls, lava tubes, and the majestic Hallasan Mountain.

Gyeongju

Known as the "Museum Without Walls," full of ancient tombs, temples like Bulguksa, and Silla Dynasty history.

The DMZ

The Demilitarized Zone. A sobering but fascinating look at the divided history of the Korean peninsula.

Incheon

A futuristic smart city, home to the global airport, Chinatown, and Songdo International Business District.

Jeonju

Famous for its Hanok Village and as the birthplace of Bibimbap. A true culinary capital.

Suwon

Home to the impressive Hwaseong Fortress, a UNESCO World Heritage site circling the city center.

K-Culture & Traditions

From the global wave of Hallyu to the spicy kick of Kimchi.

🥢

Hansik (Cuisine)

Centered on rice, vegetables, and meat. Kimchi (fermented spicy cabbage) is served at nearly every meal.

🎤

Hallyu (K-Wave)

The global popularity of South Korean culture, consisting of K-Pop (BTS, Blackpink) and K-Dramas.

👗

Hanbok

The traditional Korean attire characterised by vibrant colors and simple lines without pockets.

🙇

Respect & Etiquette

Age and hierarchy matter. Always bow when greeting and use two hands when giving or receiving items.

🥋

Taekwondo

The national sport. A martial art emphasizing head-height kicks, jumping spinning kicks, and fast footwork.

☕

Cafe Culture

Koreans love coffee. Themed cafes (from sheep cafes to 2D cartoon cafes) are major social hubs.
