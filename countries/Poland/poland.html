Must-Visit Destinations in Poland

A land of resilience, rich history, and surprising natural beauty.

Krakow

The cultural capital. Home to Europe's largest medieval market square, the Wawel Castle, and legends of dragons.

Warsaw

The Phoenix City. Rebuilt from ashes after WWII, it's now a bustling metropolis mixing skyscrapers with a restored Old Town.

Wieliczka Salt Mine

An underground kingdom. Explore chapels, chandeliers, and statues all carved entirely out of salt by miners.

Auschwitz-Birkenau

A sobering memorial and museum. A necessary visit to witness the tragic history of the Holocaust.

Zakopane

The winter capital. Nestled at the foot of the Tatra Mountains, it's popular for skiing, hiking, and unique wooden architecture.

Gdańsk

A beautiful port city on the Baltic coast. Known for its colorful Dutch-style facades, amber jewelry, and maritime history.

Malbork Castle

The largest castle in the world by land area. A massive Gothic brick fortress built by the Teutonic Knights.

Wrocław

The city of bridges and gnomes. Hunt for hundreds of tiny bronze dwarf statues hidden throughout the historic center.

Masurian Lake District

The land of a thousand lakes. A paradise for sailing, kayaking, and relaxing in nature among lush forests.

Białowieża Forest

Europe's last primeval forest. Home to the free-roaming European Bison, the heaviest land animal in Europe.

Polish Traditions

Warm hospitality, hearty food, and deep historical roots.

🥟

Pierogi

The national dish. Dumplings filled with anything from meat and sauerkraut to potato and cheese (Ruskie), or fruit.

🍞

Gościnność (Hospitality)

"Guest in the house, God in the house." Polish hospitality is legendary; you will never leave a Polish home hungry.

🎹

Frédéric Chopin

Poland's greatest composer. In Warsaw, you can sit on benches that play his music or attend free Sunday concerts in the park.

🥣

Bar Mleczny

"Milk Bars." Communist-era cafeterias that serve affordable, homemade traditional Polish food. A cult experience.

💦

Śmigus-Dyngus

Wet Monday. An Easter tradition where everyone splashes water on each other for good luck (and fun).
