Must-Visit Destinations in Vietnam

A dragon-shaped country full of natural wonders, deep history, and culinary delights.

Ha Long Bay

Bay of Descending Dragons. Cruise through thousands of towering limestone islands topping emerald waters. UNESCO site.

Hoi An

Ancient Town. Famous for its yellow colonial buildings, colorful silk lanterns, and tailors who make custom clothes in a day.

Ho Chi Minh City

Saigon. The energetic commercial hub involves French colonial architecture, war museums, and vibrant nightlife.

Hanoi

The Capital. Navigate the chaotic motorcycle traffic of the Old Quarter, visit the Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum, and sip egg coffee.

Phong Nha-Ke Bang

Cave Kingdom. Home to Son Doong, the world's largest cave. A paradise for spelunkers and jungle trekkers.

Hue

The Imperial City. The former royal capital features a massive citadel, royal tombs, and the Perfume River.

Sa Pa

High in the mountains. Trek through misty rice terraces, meet ethnic minority tribes in colorful dress, and climb Mount Fansipan.

Mekong Delta

The Rice Bowl. A maze of rivers, swamps, and islands. Take a boat tour to see floating markets and fruit orchards.

Golden Bridge

Da Nang (Ba Na Hills). Walk along the famous pedestrian bridge held up by two giant stone hands in the clouds.

Phu Quoc Island

Tropical Paradise. Famous for white sand beaches, clear waters, and the world's longest cable car ride over the sea.

Vietnamese Life

A culture built on resilience, family values, and incredible flavors.

☕

Coffee Culture

Vietnam is the world's #2 coffee producer. Try *Cà phê sữa đá* (iced milk coffee) or Hanoi's famous Egg Coffee.

🛵

Motorbike Chaos

Crossing the street is an extreme sport. Walk slowly and steadily; bikes will flow around you like water.

👗

Áo Dài

The national dress. A long, split silk tunic worn over trousers. Elegant and timeless, often white for students.

🍜

Street Food

Plastic stools on the sidewalk are where the best meals happen. Phở (soup), Bánh Mì (baguette), and Bún Chả.

🧧

Tết (Lunar New Year)

The biggest holiday. Families reunite, clean houses, give lucky money (Li Xi), and eat Bánh Chưng (sticky rice cake).
