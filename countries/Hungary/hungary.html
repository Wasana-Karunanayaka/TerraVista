Must-Visit Destinations in Hungary

From the Pearl of the Danube to the Great Plain.

Budapest

The stunning capital. See the Gothic Parliament, cross the Chain Bridge, and visit Fisherman's Bastion.

Thermal Baths

Relax in the medicinal waters of Széchenyi or Gellért. Budapest is the "City of Spas."

Lake Balaton

The "Hungarian Sea." A massive freshwater lake perfect for sailing, swimming, and wine tasting.

Eger

Famous for its castle (heroic 1552 siege), baroque architecture, and "Bull's Blood" red wine.

Tokaj

A UNESCO region producing the world's oldest botrytized wine, Tokaji Aszú (Wine of Kings).

Szentendre

An artists' village near Budapest with colorful houses, cobbled streets, and marzipan museums.

Hortobágy (Puszta)

The Great Hungarian Plain. Home to the Csikós (horsemen), grey cattle, and the Nine-Arched Bridge.

Pécs

A multicultural city with Early Christian Mausoleums (UNESCO), mosques from the Ottoman era, and ceramics.

Magyar Culture

A unique language, spicy food, and a history of invention.

🌶

Paprika & Gulyás

Paprika is the national spice. Goulash is a rich soup (not stew) of meat and vegetables seasoned with it.

🍻

Ruin Bars

Dilapidated buildings in Budapest's Jewish Quarter turned into eclectic, vibrant bars (e.g., Szimpla Kert).

🧩

Inventions

Hungarians invented the Rubik's Cube, the ballpoint pen (Bíró), and Vitamin C discovery.

🧵

Folk Art

Matyó embroidery (UNESCO) features brilliant floral motifs, especially roses, on traditional clothing.

🎻

Music (Liszt & Kodály)

From the Rhapsodies of Franz Liszt to authentic Roma folk music, melody is in the Hungarian soul.

Magyar is a Uralic language, unrelated to most European languages. It's difficult but beautiful.
