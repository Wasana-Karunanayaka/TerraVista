Magical Realism

From the Andes to the Caribbean, Colombia is a country of incredible diversity.

Cartagena

The Jewel of the Caribbean. A preserved colonial walled city with colorful balconies, cobblestone streets, and a vibrant night life.

Bogotá

The Capital. A high-altitude metropolis. Visit the Gold Museum, climb Monserrate for views, and explore the historic La Candelaria.

Medellín

City of Eternal Spring. Famous for its flower festival, cable cars connecting neighborhoods, and impressive urban transformation.

Coffee Cultural Landscape

Eje Cafetero. Stay in a coffee hacienda, taste the world's best mild coffee, and hike among the giant wax palms in Cocora Valley.

Tayrona National Park

Jungle meets Ocean. Pristine beaches backed by rain forest and the Sierra Nevada mountains. Sleep in a hammock under the stars.

San Andrés

The Sea of Seven Colors. A Caribbean island paradise famous for snorkeling, reggae culture, and duty-free shopping.

Salt Cathedral

An underground Roman Catholic church built within the tunnels of a salt mine 200 meters underground.

Guatapé

The most colorful town in the world. Climb the massive El Peñol rock for a stunning view of the surrounding lakes and islands.

Rosario Islands

Archipelago paradise. Crystal clear turquoise water, white sands, and coral reefs perfect for a day trip from Cartagena.

Colombian Vibrance

A culture defined by music, dance, and resilience.

💃

Rhythm (Cumbia & Salsa)

Music is everywhere. Cali is the world capital of Salsa, while Cumbia is the folkloric heartbeat of the coast.

🎉

Barranquilla Carnival

One of the biggest carnivals in the world. A UNESCO Masterpiece of heritage, full of color, costumes, and folklore.

💥

Tejo

The national sport. It involves throwing metal discs at paper triangles filled with gunpowder to create explosions. Usually played with beer.

💎

Emeralds

Colombia produces 70-90% of the world's emeralds. The gem trade is a deep part of the country's history and economy.

🍍

Palenqueras

Women in colorful dresses balancing bowls of fruit on their heads. They are symbols of San Basilio de Palenque, the first free slave town in the Americas.
