Must-Visit Destinations in Egypt

Home to one-third of the world's ancient antiquities and endless sunshine.

Great Pyramids of Giza

The last surviving Wonder of the Ancient World. See the Sphinx and the massive tombs of Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure.

Luxor Temple & Karnak

The world's greatest open-air museum. Walk through the Hypostyle Hall and see the Avenue of Sphinxes.

Valley of the Kings

Descend into the intricate, colorful tombs of New Kingdom pharaohs, including Tutankhamun and Seti I.

Abu Simbel

The colossal rock temples of Ramses II, famously relocated block by block to save them from the rising Nile.

Aswan & Philae Temple

Egypt's Nubian soul. Sail on a felucca around Elephantine Island and visit the beautiful Temple of Isis.

Red Sea Coast

From Hurghada to Sharm El Sheikh. World-class diving and snorkeling reefs teeming with marine life.

Alexandria

The Pearl of the Mediterranean, founded by Alexander the Great. Visit the modern Library and the Citadel of Qaitbay.

Khan el-Khalili

Cairo's oldest bazaar. A labyrinth of spices, lanterns, jewelry, and ancient architecture.

Siwa Oasis

A remote, magical oasis near the Libyan border famous for its date palms, salt lakes, and oracle temple.

Life on the Nile

A mix of ancient Pharaonic heritage, Coptic tradition, and Islamic culture.

☕

Ahwa (Coffee House)

The center of social life. Men gather to drink strong Turkish coffee, play backgammon (Tawla), and smoke shisha.

🍜

Koshary

The national dish. A carb-heavy mix of rice, macaroni, lentils, and chickpeas topped with spicy tomato sauce.

🌙

Ramadan

A special time in Egypt. Streets are decorated with Fanoos (lanterns) and come alive at night after Iftar.

🤝

The Art of Bargaining

In the souq, the first price is never the real price. Negotiating is expected, social, and done with a smile.

💃

Raqs Sharqi

Egyptian belly dance is a classical art form with deep roots, often performed at weddings and celebrations.
