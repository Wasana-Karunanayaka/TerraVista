Must-Visit Destinations in Sweden

From the archipelago of the capital to the frozen tundra of the Arctic Circle.

Stockholm

The capital built on 14 islands. Wander the cobbled streets of Gamla Stan and see the recovered warship at the Vasa Museum.

Gothenburg

The friendly west coast city. Home to Liseberg amusement park, canals, and the gateway to a stunning archipelago.

Kiruna & The Icehotel

In Swedish Lapland. Sleep in a room made of ice, go dog sledding, and hunt for the Northern Lights.

Visby, Gotland

A UNESCO medieval walled town on the island of Gotland. Known for its roses, ruins, and summer parties.

Abisko National Park

Considered one of the best places on Earth to see the Aurora Borealis due to its "Blue Hole" clear skies.

Malmö

A modern, multicultural city connected to Denmark by the Öresund Bridge. See the twisted Turning Torso skyscraper.

Uppsala

A historic university city. View the largest cathedral in Scandinavia and the burial mounds of Viking kings.

Drottningholm Palace

The private residence of the Swedish royal family. A UNESCO site with magnificent gardens and a theater.

Åre

Scandinavia's top ski resort. Offers world-class slopes in winter and downhill mountain biking in summer.

Swedish Lifestyle

Balanced, nature-loving, and fueled by strong coffee.

☕

Fika

An institution, not just a coffee break. It means sitting down with coffee and a cinnamon bun (Kanelbulle) to socialize.

🌸

Midsommar

The most important holiday. Swedes wear flower crowns, dance around the maypole like frogs, and eat herring.

⚖

Lagom

The Swedish philosophy of life: "Not too little, not too much. Just right." Moderation and balance in all things.

🏕

Allemansrätten

"The Right of Public Access." You are free to roam, camp, and pick berries anywhere in nature, even on private land.

🥦

Smörgåsbord

A festive buffet style meal. Staples include pickled herring, gravlax (cured salmon), and of course, meatballs.
