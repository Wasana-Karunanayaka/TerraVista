The Great White North

Vast wilderness, cosmopolitan cities, and endless adventure.

Banff National Park

Alberta. The crown jewel of the Rockies. Famous for the turquoise Lake Louise, Moraine Lake, and endless hiking trails.

Niagara Falls

Ontario. One of the world's most impressive waterfalls. Take the Hornblower cruise into the mist or see the view from Skylon Tower.

Vancouver

British Columbia. A stunning coastal city surrounded by mountains. Bike through Stanley Park or explore Granville Island Market.

Old Quebec City

Quebec. North America's only walled city north of Mexico. Walk the cobblestone streets and see the Château Frontenac.

Toronto

Ontario. Canada's largest city. Visit the iconic CN Tower, the Royal Ontario Museum, and the vibrant Kensington Market.

Whistler

British Columbia. world-class ski resort and host of the 2010 Winter Olympics. Great for skiing, mountain biking, and bear watching.

Peggy's Cove

Nova Scotia. A picturesque fishing village famous for its classic red-and-white lighthouse perched on granite rocks.

Churchill

Manitoba. The "Polar Bear Capital of the World." Take a tundra buggy tour to see polar bears in the wild or see Beluga whales.

Bay of Fundy

New Brunswick. Home to the highest tides in the world. Walk on the ocean floor at low tide and kayak at high tide.

Canadian Culture

Diverse, polite, and deeply connected to nature.

🏒

Ice Hockey

More than a sport; it's a religion. From frozen ponds to the NHL, hockey unites the nation.

🍁

Liquid Gold

Canada produces 71% of the world's maple syrup. Visit a "Sugar Shack" (Cabane à Sucre) for taffy on snow.

🤝

The "Sorry" Culture

Canadians are famously polite. Saying "sorry" is a reflex, used even when someone else bumps into you.

🗽

Indigenous Heritage

First Nations, Inuit, and Métis cultures are integral. Discover Totem poles, Pow Wows, and Inuit art.

☕

Tim Hortons

The iconic coffee chain. Asking for a "Double-Double" (two creams, two sugars) is a uniquely Canadian phrase.
