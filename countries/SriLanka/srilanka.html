Must-Visit Destinations in Sri Lanka

An island compact in size but infinite in variety, from ancient ruins to misty mountains.

Sigiriya

The "Lion Rock." An ancient rock fortress and palace built by King Kashyapa, famous for its frescoes and mirror wall.

Ella

A backpacker's paradise in the hill country. Famous for the Nine Arch Bridge, hiking Little Adam's Peak, and the scenic train ride.

Mirissa

A stunning beach town on the south coast, world-famous for Blue Whale watching and coconut tree hills.

Galle Fort

A UNESCO World Heritage site. A perfectly preserved colonial Dutch fort city with chic cafes and rampart walks.

Yala National Park

The best place to spot leopards in the wild. Also home to elephants, sloth bears, and crocodiles.

Kandy

The cultural capital. Home to the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic and the Esala Perahera festival.

Nuwara Eliya

"Little England." A cool climate city surrounded by miles of rolling tea plantations and colonial bungalows.

Polonnaruwa

The ancient royal capital (11th Century). Explore the well-preserved ruins of palaces, stupas, and Buddha statues.

Island Heritage

A culture shaped by Buddhism, colonial history, and tropical abundance.

🍻

Ceylon Tea

Hand-picked from the misty hills. It's the lifeblood of the economy and a daily ritual.

🏏

Cricket

More than a sport, it's a religion. Stop by any street or beach to see a match in progress.

🎣

Stilt Fishing

An iconic traditional method where fishermen balance on poles in the shallow waters of the south coast.

🧘

Ayurveda

Ancient holistic healing using herbs, oils, and massages. Wellness retreats are found island-wide.

🐘

Esala Perahera

One of the oldest Buddhist festivals featuring fire-dancers, drummers, and elaborately decorated elephants.
