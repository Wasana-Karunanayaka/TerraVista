The Wild Heart of Africa

Home of the Safari and the cradle of mankind.

Masai Mara

The greatest wildlife show on earth. Witness the annual Great Migration of wildebeest and zebra, and see the Big Five.

Amboseli National Park

Famous for huge herds of free-ranging elephants and the best views of Mt. Kilimanjaro (across the border in Tanzania).

Nairobi

The Safari Capital. Visit the David Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage, the Giraffe Centre, and the Nairobi National Park (city safari).

Diani Beach

Relaxation. Voted Africa's leading beach destination. White sands, turquoise waters, and coral reefs south of Mombasa.

Mount Kenya

The second highest peak in Africa. A UNESCO site offering challenging trekking through diverse vegetation zones.

Lake Nakuru

The Pink Lake. Famous for the thousands of flamingos that line its shores and its rhino sanctuary.

Lamu Island

Swahili Culture. Kenya's oldest living town. No cars, only donkeys. A peaceful blend of Arabic, Indian, and African influences.

Tsavo East & West

The Theater of the Wild. Massive parks known for red elephants, man-eating lion history, and the Mzima Springs.

Hell's Gate

Adventure. One of the few parks where you can cycle or walk among wildlife. The scenery inspired "The Lion King."

Kenyan Pride

A land of 42 tribes and "Harambee".

🛡

Maasai Warriors

Known for their red shukas (robes), beadwork, and jumping dance (Adumu). They have maintained traditions in the face of modernity.

🦁

The Big Five

Lion, Leopard, Rhino, Elephant, Buffalo. Safaris are not just tourism; conservation is a central national pillar.

🏃

Long Distance Running

The Kalenjin tribe produces the world's best marathon runners. Running is a path to glory and national pride.

🚌

Matatus

Privately owned minibuses. They are flamboyant, covered in graffiti art, play loud music, and are the pulse of Nairobi streets.

🤝

Harambee

"All pull together." The official national motto. It signifies community self-help events to build schools or help neighbors.
