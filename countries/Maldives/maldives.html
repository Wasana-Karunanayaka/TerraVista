Must-Visit Atolls & Islands

From private luxury resorts to charming local fishing villages.

Mal√©

One of the world's most densely populated cities. Visit the Golden Dome Friday Mosque, the fish market, and Sultan Park.

Baa Atoll

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. Famous for its incredible biodiversity, coral reefs, and luxury eco-resorts.

Maafushi

The heart of budget tourism. A local island with guesthouses, water sports, and a "Bikini Beach" for tourists.

Ari Atoll

The best place to spot Whale Sharks year-round. Crystal clear waters ideal for scuba diving and snorkeling.

Sea of Stars (Vaadhoo)

Witness the magical bioluminescent plankton that lights up the shoreline like a starry night sky.

Hanifaru Bay

A sanctuary for manta rays. In season, hundreds of mantas gather here to feed in a cyclonic frenzy.

Banana Reef

The first dive site discovered in the Maldives. Named for its shape, it's packed with colorful fish and soft corals.

Utheemu Ganduvaru

The wooden palace of Sultan Mohamed Thakurufaanu, a national hero who fought the Portuguese. Rich in history.

Addu Atoll

The southernmost atoll. Heart-shaped and unique for its British colonial history and causeway-connected islands.

Overwater Bungalows

The iconic Maldivian experience. Stay in a luxury villa perched on stilts directly over the turquoise lagoon.

Maldivian Way of Life

Beyond the resorts lies a rich culture of seafarers and craftsmen.

🥁

Bodu Beru

"Big Drums." The soul of Maldivian music. A rhythmic dance performance that builds to a trance-like crescendo.

🐟

Mas Huni

The typical breakfast. Finely chopped tuna mixed with onion, coconut, and chili, eaten with Roshi (flatbread).

⛵

The Dhoni

The traditional handcrafted fishing boat. Its curve captures the elegance of the islands' maritime heritage.

🎨

Liyelaa Jehun

Traditional lacquer work. Strange but beautiful vases and boxes carved from wood and painted in red, yellow, and black.

🕌

Island Life

The Maldives is 100% Muslim. Local islands have a slower pace, alcohol is prohibited, and modesty is respected.
