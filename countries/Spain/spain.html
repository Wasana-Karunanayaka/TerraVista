Must-Visit Destinations in Spain

A tapestry of regions, each with its own flavor, history, and architectural marvels.

Sagrada Família

Barcelona. Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece. A basilica that looks like it was grown from nature, not built.

The Alhambra

Granada. A stunning palace and fortress complex that showcases the finest Islamic architecture in Europe.

Park Güell

Another Gaudí whimsical wonder in Barcelona. Famous for its colorful mosaic salamander and undulating benches.

Seville Cathedral

The largest Gothic cathedral in the world. Climb the Giralda tower (originally a minaret) for city views.

El Prado Museum

Madrid. One of the world's finest art collections, featuring masters like Velázquez, Goya, and El Greco.

Ibiza

The White Isle. World-famous for its nightlife, but also for its tranquil yoga retreats and stunning coves.

Great Mosque of Córdoba

La Mezquita. A dazzling forest of candy-cane arches inside a mosque that was converted into a cathedral at its center.

Toledo

The City of Three Cultures. Christians, Muslims, and Jews lived here in peace. A living museum of medieval swords and art.

Costa del Sol

The Sun Coast. Malaga and Marbella offer endless beaches, sunshine, and luxury resorts on the Mediterranean.

Santiago de Compostela

The final stop of the Camino de Santiago pilgrimage. The cathedral holds the shrine of St. James the Apostle.

Spanish Life

Life is lived outdoors, often late into the night, revolving around food and family.

Flamenco

Born in Andalusia. A passionate art form combining singing (cante), dance (baile), and guitar (toque).

🛌

The Siesta

The afternoon break. Shops may close from 2-5 PM during the hottest part of the day, allowing for a long lunch and rest.

🥘

Paella

The pride of Valencia. Saffron-infused rice cooked with rabbit, chicken, or seafood in a wide pan. Never eat it for dinner!

🎉

Fiestas

Spain loves to party. From the tomato fight of La Tomatina to the Running of the Bulls, festivals are wild and colorful.
