Arctic Magic & Lakeland

A land of stark beauty, from the frozen north to the serene southern lakes.

Helsinki

The White City of the North. Famous for its design district, the white cathedral, and the Temppeliaukio Rock Church.

Rovaniemi

The Official Hometown of Santa Claus. Cross the Arctic Circle, meet reindeer, and visit the Santa Claus Village year-round.

Aurora Borealis

Lapland. One of the best places on Earth to see the Northern Lights. Stay in a glass igloo in Kakslauttanen for a view from bed.

Finnish Lakeland

Europe's largest lake district. A labyrinth of thousands of blue lakes, islands, and summer cottages (mökki).

Suomenlinna

Helsinki. A massive 18th-century sea fortress built on six islands. A UNESCO World Heritage site and popular picnic spot.

Nuuksio National Park

Wilderness near the capital. Dense forests, marked trails, and lakes, perfect for experiencing the Finnish connection to nature.

Turku

The oldest city in Finland. Known for its medieval castle, cathedral, and the beautiful Turku Archipelago with 20,000 islands.

Porvoo

A charming medieval town famous for its red wooden warehouses along the riverside and cobblestone streets.

Levi

The largest ski resort in Finland. Offers world-class skiing, husky safaris, and snowmobiling under the midnight sun or polar night.

The Finnish Way

Sisu, Sauna, and silence.

♨

Sauna Culture

With 3 million saunas for 5.5 million people, it's a way of life. It's a place for cleansing, socializing, and major decisions.

💪

Sisu

A unique Finnish concept of stoic determination, grit, and bravery in the face of adversity. It's the national spirit.

☕

Coffee Consumption

Finns drink more coffee per capita than anyone else in the world. Served black and often, usually with a sweet bun (Pulla).

🌲

Everyman's Right

Jokamiehenoikeus. You can roam freely in nature, forage for berries and mushrooms, and camp anywhere, even on private land.

🎸

Heavy Metal

Surprisingly, Finland has the most heavy metal bands per capita. Bands like Nightwish and HIM are national treasures.
