Must-Visit Destinations in Thailand

From the chaotic energy of Bangkok to the serene islands of the Andaman Sea.

Grand Palace

Bangkok. The spiritual heart of the Kingdom. A dazzling complex of gold spires and the home of the Emerald Buddha.

Chiang Mai

The Rose of the North. Famous for its misty mountains, hundreds of ancient temples, and ethical elephant sanctuaries.

Phi Phi Islands

The ultimate tropical paradise. Turquoise waters, limestone cliffs, and the famous Maya Bay (The Beach).

Wat Arun

The Temple of Dawn. An iconic riverside temple in Bangkok encrusted with colorful porcelain. Best viewed at sunset.

Ayutthaya

The ancient capital. Explore the UNESCO ruins of the Siamese kingdom, including the Buddha head trapped in tree roots.

Railay Beach

Krabi. Accessible only by boat. Famous for its towering limestone karsts which attract rock climbers from around the globe.

Khao Sok National Park

A jungle older than the Amazon. Stay in a floating bungalow on Cheow Lan Lake and spot wild elephants and gibbons.

Damnoen Saduak

The famous floating market. Boats piled high with tropical fruits, coconut pancakes, and noodles navigate the canals.

Sukhothai

Dawn of Happiness. The first capital of Siam. Rent a bike and cycle through the peaceful historical park.

Pai

A bohemian mountain village. Known for its relaxed vibe, waterfalls, hot springs, and Pai Canyon sunsets.

Thai Culture

Deeply spiritual, famously friendly, and obsessed with food.

🍜

Street Food

Bangkok is the street food capital of the world. From Pad Thai to Mango Sticky Rice, it's cheap, safe, and delicious.

🙏

The Wai

The traditional greeting. Palms pressed together with a slight bow. It shows respect ("Sawasdee"). Use it interacting with elders.

🕊

Buddhism

95% of Thais are Buddhist. Monks in orange robes are revered. Always dress modestly (cover shoulders/knees) in temples.

🥊

Muay Thai

The Art of Eight Limbs. The national sport utilizing fists, elbows, knees, and shins. Watch a match at Lumpinee Stadium.

💦

Songkran

Thai New Year (April). The world's biggest water fight. Originally a cleansing ritual, now a nationwide party.

Thai Phrasebook

Tones are hard, but a smile goes a long way. End sentences with "Khrup" (male) or "Ka" (female).
