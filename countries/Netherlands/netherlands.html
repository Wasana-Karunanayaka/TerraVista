Must-Visit Destinations in the Netherlands

Beyond Amsterdam lies a country of rich history, modern art, and ingenious water management.

Amsterdam Canals

The Grachtengordel. Cruise the UNESCO-listed 17th-century canals, lined with narrow merchant houses and houseboats.

Keukenhof Gardens

The Garden of Europe. Visit in spring to see millions of tulips, hyacinths, and daffodils in full bloom.

Rijksmuseum

Home to the Dutch Masters. See Rembrandt's "The Night Watch" and Vermeer's "The Milkmaid" in person.

Kinderdijk Windmills

A UNESCO site featuring 19 innovative 18th-century windmills built to drain the polders and keep the land dry.

Van Gogh Museum

Dedicated to the works of Vincent van Gogh and his contemporaries. Contains the largest collection of his paintings.

Zaanse Schans

A living open-air museum. See working windmills, wooden clog workshops, and cheese farms just north of Amsterdam.

Giethoorn

"The Venice of the North." A car-free village where transport is only by boat on the peaceful canals.

Rotterdam

A modern powerhouse. Famous for its futuristic architecture, the Cube Houses, and the massive Erasmus Bridge.

The Hague

The seat of government and the King. Visit the Binnenhof, the Mauritshuis museum, and Scheveningen beach.

Dutch Culture

Tolerant, direct, and famously flat (the country, not the culture!).

🚲

Cycling (Fietsen)

The preferred mode of transport for everyone from toddlers to the Prime Minister. There are over 35,000km of bike paths.

🧀

Gouda & Edam

Dutch cheese is world-famous. Visit the traditional cheese markets in Alkmaar or Gouda to see wheels being weighed.

👑

King's Day (Koningsdag)

April 27th. The whole country turns orange. Streets become flea markets (vrijmarkt) and parties erupt on canals.

🥞

Stroopwafel

The ultimate treat. Two thin waffles stuck together with caramel syrup. Best eaten warm over a coffee cup.

🗣

Dutch Directness

The Dutch value honesty and directness. They speak their mind ("bespreekbaarheid"), which is not meant to be rude.
