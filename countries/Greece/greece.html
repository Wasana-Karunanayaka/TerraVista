Islands & Antiquity

From the Acropolis to the deep blue Aegean Sea.

Athens

The Capital. Dominated by the 5th-century BC landmarks like the Acropolis and Parthenon. A bustling mix of ancient and modern.

Santorini

The honeymoon island. Famous for its whitewashed cubic buildings with blue domes, stunning sunsets in Oia, and volcanic beaches.

Meteora

A surreal landscape of immense natural rock pillars with Eastern Orthodox monasteries precariously perched on top.

Mykonos

The party island. Known for its summer party atmosphere, iconic windmills, Little Venice, and luxury beach clubs.

Delphi

The Navel of the Earth. Once the home of the Oracle of Apollo, it is one of the most important archaeological sites in Greece.

Crete

The largest island. Home to the Minoan Palace of Knossos, pink sand beaches like Elafonissi, and rugged gorges.

Zakynthos

Navigation Island. Famous for "Navagio" (Shipwreck Beach), spectacular blue caves, and loggerhead sea turtles.

Rhodes

The Island of the Knights. Features one of the best-preserved medieval towns in Europe and the ancient Acropolis of Lindos.

Olympia

Birthplace of the Olympic Games. See the ancient stadium, the Temple of Zeus, and the spot where the Olympic flame is lit.

Hellenic Spirit

Philoxenia, Food, and Philosophy.

🤝

Philoxenia

Literally "friend of the stranger." A deep-rooted ancient tradition of hospitality where guests are treated like royalty.

☕

Frappé & Freddo

Coffee is a ritual. Greeks spend hours sipping iced Frappés or Freddo Espressos at cafes, debating politics and life.

🏛

Mythology

The stories of Zeus, Athena, and Hercules aren't just fairy tales; they are woven into the names of streets, ferries, and companies.

💃

Panigiria

Traditional village festivals usually held in summer. There is live music, circle dancing (Ikariotikos), and endless food and wine.

🥘

Mezedes & Ouzo

Sharing small plates (tzatziki, calamari, feta) accompanied by Ouzo or Tsipouro is the standard way to eat socially.
