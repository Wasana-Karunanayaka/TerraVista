Aztecs, Mayans, and Beaches

A diverse land where history meets paradise.

Chichen Itza

One of the New Seven Wonders of the World. A massive Mayan city featuring the iconic El Castillo pyramid (Temple of Kukulcan).

Mexico City (CDMX)

The cultural heart. Built on an ancient lake, it offers world-class museums, the Zócalo, and endless culinary adventures.

Riviera Maya

Cancun, Playa del Carmen, and Tulum. Famous for turquoise Caribbean waters, cenotes (underground swimming holes), and beachfront ruins.

Teotihuacan

City of the Gods. Climb the massive Pyramids of the Sun and Moon in this ancient holy city near Mexico City.

Oaxaca

The culinary capital. Known for its 7 Moles, mezcal, colorful colonial architecture, and indigenous festivals like Guelaguetza.

Copper Canyon

Barranca del Cobre. A series of massive canyons in the Sierra Madre, deeper than the Grand Canyon. Ride the famous "El Chepe" train.

Cabo San Lucas

Where the desert meets the sea. Famous for "El Arco" (The Arch), luxury resorts, deep-sea fishing, and nightlife.

Guanajuato

A colorful UNESCO World Heritage city built into a ravine. Famous for its underground tunnels, mummies, and arts festival.

Puerto Vallarta

A resort town on the Pacific coast known for its beaches, water sports, and nightlife scene on the Malecon boardwalk.

Mexican Soul

A fusion of Indigenous and Spanish roots.

💀

Día de los Muertos

Day of the Dead (Nov 1-2). A colorful celebration honoring deceased loved ones with altars (ofrendas), marigolds, and sugar skulls.

🌮

UNESCO Cuisine

Mexican food is UNESCO-protected heritage. Go beyond tacos to taste Mole, Pozole, Tamales, and Chiles en Nogada.

🎸

Mariachi Music

The sound of Mexico. Bands with violins, trumpets, and guitars dressed in charro suits, singing songs of love and country.

🤼

Lucha Libre

Free Wrestling. High-flying maneuvers and colorful masks. It's a pop-culture phenomenon and a battle between Good (Técnicos) and Evil (Rudos).

🎨

Alebrijes & Art

From the surrealist art of Frida Kahlo to the brightly colored folk art sculptures called Alebrijes.
