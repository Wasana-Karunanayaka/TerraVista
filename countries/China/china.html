Must-Visit Destinations in China

A land of epic proportions, from ancient wonders to futuristic skylines.

The Great Wall

One of the greatest feats of human engineering. Hike the Mutianyu or Jinshanling sections for breathtaking views.

Forbidden City

The world's largest palace complex. The home of 24 emperors, located in the heart of Beijing across from Tiananmen Square.

Terracotta Warriors

Xi'an. An underground army of 8,000 life-sized soldiers, horses, and chariots built to protect Emperor Qin Shi Huang.

The Bund, Shanghai

A dazzling waterfront promenade showcasing colonial architecture on one side and the futuristic Lujiazui skyline on the other.

Li River & Karst Mountains

A landscape immortalized in Chinese painting. Cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo past dramatic limestone peaks.

Chengdu Panda Base

The best place in the world to see Giant Pandas and Red Pandas in their natural habitat. Also famous for spicy hotpot.

Zhangjiajie

Home of the "Avatar Mountains." Towering sandstone pillars that seem to float in the mist.

Summer Palace

Beijing's royal garden. A masterpiece of Chinese landscape garden design with a vast lake, temples, and the Marble Boat.

Yellow Mountain

Celebrated in art and literature for its oddly shaped pines, granite peaks, and the "Sea of Clouds" phenomenon.

Chinese Traditions

A civilization that celebrates harmony, respect, and exquisite craftsmanship.

🍵

Tea Ceremony (Gongfu Cha)

An art form involving the ritual preparation and presentation of tea. It emphasizes mindfulness and respect.

🖌

Calligraphy

Regarded as the highest visual art form. The brush strokes reveal the character and cultivation of the writer.

🥋

Kung Fu

Traditional martial arts born in the Shaolin Temple. It combines physical discipline with spiritual philosophy.

🦲

Spring Festival (New Year)

The most important holiday. Marked by family reunions, red envelopes (Hongbao), dumplings, and fireworks.

🥙

Culinary Arts

From the delicate Dim Sum of the south to the crispy Peking Duck of the north and the fiery spice of Sichuan.
