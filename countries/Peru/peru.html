Must-Visit Destinations in Peru

A country of extreme biodiversity, from the dry coastal deserts to the high Andes and lush Amazon.

Machu Picchu

The Lost City of the Incas. Hidden in the misty Andes, this wonder of the world is a testament to ancient engineering.

Cusco

The Imperial City. Once the capital of the Inca Empire, now a stunning blend of Inca walls and Spanish colonial architecture.

The Sacred Valley

Urubamba. Dotted with ancient citadel ruins like Pisac and Ollantaytambo, and colorful local markets.

Rainbow Mountain

Vinicunca. A geological marvel where mineral deposits have striped the mountain in seven vibrant colors.

Lake Titicaca

The highest navigable lake in the world. Home to the Uros people who live on floating islands made of dried reeds.

Amazon Rainforest

Enter via Puerto Maldonado or Iquitos. Explore the lungs of the Earth, incredible wildlife, and jungle lodges.

Huacachina

A desert oasis west of Ica. A village surrounding a green lagoon, surrounded by massive sand dunes perfect for sandboarding.

Nazca Lines

Mysterious ancient geoglyphs etched into the desert floor. Best viewed from a small plane to see the hummingbird, monkey, and spider.

Colca Canyon

Deeper than the Grand Canyon. The best place to watch gigantic Andean Condors soar on thermal currents.

Lima

The Gastronomic Capital of the Americas. Visit the historic center, the seaside district of Miraflores, and world-class restaurants.

Peruvian Heritage

A fusion of Pre-Columbian roots, Spanish influence, and modern flair.

🍽

Ceviche

The national dish. Raw fish cured in fresh lime juice, spiced with ají peppers, and served with corn (choclo) and sweet potato.

🧶

Andean Textiles

Woven by hand using alpaca or llama wool. The intricate patterns tell stories of the community and the cosmos.

🌍

Pachamama

Mother Earth. Indigenous spirituality deeply respects the land. Offerings (pagos) are still made to ask for protection and harvest.

🦕

Camelids

Llamas were pack animals for the Incas; Alpacas are prized for their soft wool. You will see them everywhere in the highlands.

☀

Inti Raymi

The Festival of the Sun. Reenacted every June solstice in Cusco, celebrating the Inca sun god with massive processions.
