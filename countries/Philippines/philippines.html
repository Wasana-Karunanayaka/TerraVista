Must-Visit Destinations in the Philippines

A tropical paradise with some of the world's best beaches, diving spots, and natural wonders.

Palawan

Consistently voted the "Best Island in the World." Crystal clear lagoons, limestone cliffs, and hidden beaches in El Nido and Coron.

Boracay

Famous for its powdery White Beach, vibrant nightlife, and stunning sunsets. A reinvigorated island paradise.

Chocolate Hills

Over 1,200 symmetrical hills in Bohol that turn brown in the dry season. Also home to the tiny Tarsier primate.

Banaue Rice Terraces

The "Eighth Wonder of the World." 2,000-year-old terraces carved into the mountains of Ifugao by ancestors.

Cebu

The "Queen City of the South." Swim with whale sharks in Oslob, canyoneer in Kawasan Falls, and taste the best Lechon.

Siargao

The Surfing Capital. Known for the legendary Cloud 9 break, coconut tree-lined roads, and a laid-back island vibe.

Intramuros

The historic Walled City within Manila. Spanish colonial architecture, Fort Santiago, and horse-drawn carriages (Kalesa).

Mayon Volcano

Renowned for its "Perfect Cone" shape. An active volcano in Albay serving as a breathtaking backdrop.

Vigan

A UNESCO Heritage city. One of the few surviving Hispanic towns in Asia, famous for its preserved Calle Crisologo.

Filipino Spirit

"Bayanihan" — The spirit of communal unity, work, and cooperation.

🚌

Jeepneys

The "King of the Road." Colorful, kitschy, and crowded public transport originally made from US military jeeps.

🎉

Fiestas

Every town has a fiesta. Sinulog (Cebu) and Ati-Atihan are massive street parties celebrating the Santo Niño.

🍗

Adobo & Lechon

Adobo (marinated stew) is the unofficial national dish. Lechon (roasted pig) is the star of every celebration.

🍧

Halo-Halo

"Mix-Mix." The ultimate shaved ice dessert with beans, fruits, jellies, flan, and ube (purple yam) ice cream.

🤝

Hospitality

Filipinos are known as the warmest people in Asia. You will always be greeted with a smile and offer of food.
