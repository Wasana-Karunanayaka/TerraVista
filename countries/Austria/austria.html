Imperial Cities & Alpine Peaks

A land where history, culture, and nature blend in perfect harmony.

Vienna (Wien)

The Capital. Walk in the footsteps of emperors at Schönbrunn Palace, see Klimt's 'The Kiss' at Belvedere, and enjoy a coffee house.

Salzburg

The Stage of the World. Birthplace of Mozart and setting for "The Sound of Music," with a Baroque old town overseen by a fortress.

Hallstatt

The Pearl of Austria. A fairytale village nestled between a dark blue lake and soaring mountains. Explore the world's oldest salt mine.

Innsbruck

Capital of the Alps. Famous for the Golden Roof, futuristic architecture by Zaha Hadid, and world-class winter sports.

Grossglockner Road

Austria's highest mountain pass. A scenic drive that takes you into the heart of the Hohe Tauern National Park and glaciers.

Graz

The Culinary Capital. Austria's second city blends Renaissance courtyards with modern design like the "Friendly Alien" Kunsthaus.

Melk Abbey

A Baroque Masterpiece. This massive Benedictine abbey sits high above the Danube River in the scenic Wachau Valley.

Eisriesenwelt

World of the Ice Giants. The largest ice cave system in the world, located near Werfen. A frozen underground spectacle.

Zell am See

Lake and Glacier. A picturesque town offering crystal clear water in summer and glacier skiing on the Kitzsteinhorn in winter.

Austrian Culture

Gemütlichkeit, Tradition, and High Art.

☕

Kaffeehaus (Coffee House)

An institution. It's not just a cafe; it's a living room. Order a 'Melange', read a newspaper, and stay as long as you like.

🎼

Classical Heritage

The land of Mozart, Haydn, Schubert, and Strauss. The Vienna Philharmonic's New Year's Concert is world-famous.

💃

Viennese Ball Season

From January to March, over 400 balls take place in Vienna. The Opera Ball is the highlight of the social calendar.

⛷

Skiing & Après-Ski

Skiing is the national sport. But the party after the slopes (Après-Ski) with schnapps and Schlager music is equally important.

👿

Krampus

A darker Christmas tradition. On Dec 5th, this horned beast roams the streets to punish naughty children, contrasting St. Nicholas.
