Must-Visit Destinations in Singapore

A small island with huge attractions, from vertical gardens to colonial history.

Marina Bay Sands

The iconic ship-shaped hotel. enjoy the Skypark Observation Deck for the best views of the skyline and the Formula 1 circuit.

Gardens by the Bay

A futuristic park featuring the alien-like Supertrees, the Cloud Forest with its indoor waterfall, and the Flower Dome.

Sentosa Island

The State of Fun. Home to Universal Studios Singapore, S.E.A. Aquarium, and sandy beaches like Siloso and Palawan.

Chinatown

A historic district. Visit the Buddha Tooth Relic Temple, buy souvenirs on Pagoda Street, and eat at Maxwell Food Centre.

Little India

A vibrant explosion of color, scent, and sound. Shop at Mustafa Centre (open 24/7) and visit the Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple.

Singapore Botanic Gardens

A UNESCO World Heritage Site. Stroll through the lush greenery and see thousands of orchids at the National Orchid Garden.

Jewel Changi

The world's best airport. Famous for the Rain Vortex, the world's tallest indoor waterfall, surrounded by a forest terrace.

Singapore Zoo / Night Safari

An "open concept" zoo where animals roam in naturalistic habitats. The Night Safari offers a unique tram ride in the dark.

Kampong Gelam

The Malay heritage district. Dominated by the golden dome of Sultan Mosque and lined with trendy cafes and carpet shops on Haji Lane.

Singaporean Culture

A melting pot of Chinese, Malay, Indian, and Eurasian influences.

🍜

Hawker Culture

UNESCO Intangible Heritage. Community dining rooms serving affordable, Michelin-quality street food like Chicken Rice and Laksa.

👘

Peranakan Heritage

Straits-born Chinese. A unique hybrid culture with distinctive colorful architecture (shophouses), kebaya clothing, and spicy food.

🗣

Singlish

The colloquial English. peppered with dialect words like "Lah" (emphasis), "Kiasu" (fear of missing out), and "Chope" (reserve a seat).

🚷

The "Fine" City

Known for safety and cleanliness. Strict laws (no chewing gum sales, no durians on trains) keep the city orderly and spotless.

🥤

King of Fruits

Durian. You either love it or hate it. It smells like gym socks but tastes like creamy custard. Banned in hotels!
