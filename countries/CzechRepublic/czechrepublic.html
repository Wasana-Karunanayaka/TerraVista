Bohemian Wonders

A land dense with history, stunning architecture, and rolling hills.

Prague (Praha)

The City of a Hundred Spires. Walk the Charles Bridge, visit the largest ancient castle in the world, and see the Astronomical Clock.

Český Krumlov

A fairytale town. A UNESCO World Heritage site with a massive castle towering over a maze of winding medieval streets and the Vltava River.

Kutná Hora

The Silver City. Famous for the Sedlec Ossuary ("Bone Church") decorated with the bones of 40,000 people, and St. Barbara's Church.

Karlovy Vary

The Spa Town. Famous for its 12 main hot springs, colonnades, international film festival, and Becherovka liqueur.

Bohemian Switzerland

A national park on the German border. Known for the Pravčická brána, the largest natural sandstone arch in Europe.

Brno

The second largest city. A hub of modernist architecture (Villa Tugendhat), student life, and a vibrant cafe culture.

Plzeň (Pilsen)

The birthplace of Pilsner beer. Visit the Pilsner Urquell brewery to taste the golden lager straight from the barrel.

Karlštejn Castle

A majestic Gothic castle built by Emperor Charles IV to safe-keep the Bohemian crown jewels and holy relics.

Telč

The Venice of Moravia. A UNESCO town famous for its long town square lined with well-preserved Renaissance and Baroque houses.

Czech Traditions

Beer, puppets, and a darker sense of humor.

🍻

Beer Culture

Czechs consume the most beer per capita in the world. It's "Liquid Bread." Foam is important; a "Mlíko" pour is almost all foam.

🎭

Puppetry

Marionettes are a serious art form here. You can find elaborate puppet shops and theaters performing operas like Don Giovanni.

🍸

Bohemian Crystal

World-renowned for quality and craftsmanship. Hand-cut lead crystal and glass blowing traditions date back centuries.

🍄

Mushroom Hunting

A national obsession. In autumn, families head to the forests with baskets to pick wild mushrooms ("Houbaření").

🎈

Pomlázka

A unique Easter tradition where men lightly whip women with braided willow branches for youth and health. (It's controversial but traditional).
