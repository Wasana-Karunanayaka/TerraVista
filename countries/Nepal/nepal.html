Must-Visit Destinations in Nepal

Home to 8 of the world's 10 highest peaks and ancient spirituality.

Mount Everest Region

Sagarmatha. Trek to Everest Base Camp through Sherpa villages to stand at the foot of the highest mountain on Earth.

Kathmandu Durbar Square

The historic heart of the capital. A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its stunning Newari architecture and temples.

Pokhara

The gateway to the Annapurna Circuit. Famous for its serene Phewa Lake and the reflection of Mt. Machhapuchhre.

Chitwan National Park

The jungle lowlands. Go on a safari to spot one-horned rhinoceroses, Bengal tigers, and elephants in the wild.

Swayambhunath

The "Monkey Temple." An ancient stupa atop a hill in the Kathmandu Valley, offering panoramic views and cheeky residents.

Boudhanath Stupa

One of the largest stupas in the world. The center of Tibetan Buddhism in Nepal, surrounded by monasteries.

Lumbini

The birthplace of Lord Buddha. A sacred pilgrimage site with the Maya Devi Temple and monasteries from around the world.

Bhaktapur

"City of Devotees." Known for its well-preserved medieval art, pottery square, and the exquisite 55-Window Palace.

Annapurna Circuit

One of the wold's classic long-distance treks, circling the Annapurna Massif and crossing the Thorong La Pass.

Nagarkot

A hill station near Kathmandu famous for its sunrise views over the Himalayas, including Everest on a clear day.

Nepalese Culture

A harmonious blend of Hinduism, Buddhism, and mountain resilience.

🙏

Namaste

The universal greeting. Palms pressed together near the heart, meaning "The divine in me bows to the divine in you."

🍛

Dal Bhat Power

"Dal Bhat Power, 24 Hour!" The staple meal of lentil soup, rice, and curry eaten twice a day by locals.

🧗

The Sherpas

Renowned for their mountaineering skills and unmatched endurance at high altitudes, they are the guardians of the Himalayas.

👑

The Kumari

The Living Goddess. A young girl selected from the Newar community who is worshipped as the incarnation of Taleju.

🎌

Prayer Flags

Colorful flags (blue, white, red, green, yellow) hung in high places to spread prayers and compassion on the wind.
