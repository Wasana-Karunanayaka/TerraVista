Must-Visit Destinations in Jordan

An open-air museum of history, from Roman cities to Martian landscapes.

Petra

The "Rose City." Walk through the Siq to reveal Al-Khazneh (The Treasury). A Nabataean masterpiece carved into rock.

Wadi Rum

The Valley of the Moon. A mars-like desert protected as a UNESCO site. take a jeep tour or camp with Bedouins.

The Dead Sea

The lowest point on Earth. The water is so salty you can't sink. Apply the mineral-rich mud for a natural spa treatment.

Jerash

One of the best-preserved Roman cities in the world. Walk the Colonnaded Street and see Hadrian's Arch.

Amman Citadel

Jabal al-Qal'a. Ancient ruins sitting on the highest hill in Amman, offering panoramic views of the capital's seven hills.

Aqaba / Red Sea

Jordan's only coastal city. Famous for its warm weather, vibrant coral reefs, and world-class scuba diving.

Mount Nebo

The site where Moses is said to have viewed the Promised Land. Offers stunning views of the Holy Land on clear days.

Madaba

"The City of Mosaics." Famous for the 6th-century mosaic map of Jerusalem and the Holy Land in St. Georgeâ€™s Church.

Dana Biosphere Reserve

Jordan's largest nature reserve. A hiker's paradise with diverse geology, from sandstone cliffs to wadis.

Wadi Mujib

The "Grand Canyon of Jordan." An adventure hub for canyoning, hiking through water, and dramatic rock formations.

Jordanian Heritage

A land where guests are treated like kings.

☕

Bedouin Hospitality

"Ahlan Wa Sahlan." Offering sweet tea or cardamom coffee to strangers is a sacred duty of honor.

🍛

Mansaf

The national dish. Lamb cooked in fermented dried yogurt (Jameed) served on rice. Traditionally eaten with the right hand.

🎨

Mosaic Art

Continuing a Byzantine tradition, intricate stone mosaics are still crafted by hand in Madaba and beyond.

🚟

Wellness Traditions

Since the days of Cleopatra, the mineral-rich black mud of the Dead Sea has been used for healing and beauty.

🎆

Petra by Night

A magical experience. Walking the Siq to the Treasury illuminated only by 1,500 candles and Bedouin music.
